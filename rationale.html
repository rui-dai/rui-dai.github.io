Rationale

147,950 newly diagnosed colorectal cancer (CRC) in the US in 2020
104,610 cases of colon cancer
43,340 cases of rectal cancer. 

Most occur in individuals over 50 years, 17,930 new cases of CRC (12%) will be diagnosed in individuals aged younger than 50 years. In addition, there will be an estimated 53,200 CRC deaths in 2020, including 3640 decedents (7%) aged younger than 50 years.

CRC patient population as a whole is rapidly shifting younger, with declining incidence in older age and increasing incidence in younger pts. Median age of diagnosis has dropped from 72 yrs during 2001‐02 to 66 yrs during 2015‐16. Median age at CRC diagnosis is younger for rectal cancer (63 yrs) than for colon cancer (69 yrs) and for blacks (64 yrs) than for whites (68 yrs). There is currently a generational increase in incidence and mortality in patients < 50 years old. 

1 in 10 new CRC diagnoses involve individuals age < 50
1 in 5 affected with EOCRC carries a pathogenic germline variant in genes associated with predisposition to cancer

Most have no clinically identifiable risk factors
CRC risk among millennials to be 2-4 times higher than their grandparents
Diagnosed at later stages and often present with metastatic disease
